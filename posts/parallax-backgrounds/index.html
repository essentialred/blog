Weird Bugs

Review

This is clearly the most charming solution. It is developer-friendly, minimal code, and no JS. It should absolutely be the first one to try if you're looking for a solution to making one or two banners on your website fancy and the rest of the template is fairly static. I have made the mistake, however, of going down this road for a much more animation-heavy experience where there were many other elements moving on scroll or at fixed points, and the strange bugs listed above became prohibitive. There are also performance issues when you have a large layout employing the cover/fixed CSS solution to create the illusion of full-page panels. As soon as more elements and greater demands are made on your parallax layout, this solution becomes questionable at best. I still hope that browser evolution will catch up to us and that someday fullscreen, pure-CSS parallax panels will be butter-smooth and cooperative! Until then, read on.
